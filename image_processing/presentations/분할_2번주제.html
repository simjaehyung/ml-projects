Lighting Robustness: Diagonal vs. Axial Scaling

How does changing brightness ($k$) affect the mathematical position of colors in their 3D spaces?

The Math of Brightness

RGB Space (Diagonal vector scaling)
[R', G', B'] = k * [R, G, B] Requires 3 multiplications per pixel. Colors shift diagonally towards the $[255,255,255]$ white corner or the origin $[0,0,0]$.
HSV Space (Axial scalar scaling)
V' = k * V Requires 1 multiplication. Hue (X/Y position) is perfectly preserved. The color simply moves straight up or down the Z-axis.
